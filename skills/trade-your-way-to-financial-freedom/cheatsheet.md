# Cheatsheet — Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom

## The Hierarchy of Effort (spend time accordingly)
| Pillar | Weight | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Psychology / discipline | ~60% | Work on yourself first; biases lose more money than bad systems |
| Position sizing ("how much?") | ~30% | The lever that meets objectives; second-most important |
| System / entry ("what & when?") | ~10% | Least important — stop over-optimizing it |

## Decision Rules (if → then → because)
- **If you have no predefined stop → don't enter**, because without R you can't size, score, or limit the loss.
- **If a trade hits its stop → exit now, don't widen it**, because widening is the need-to-be-right turning a small loss into a large one.
- **If a winner is running in a trend → trail, don't take a fixed small profit**, because capping winners starves the system of the big-R trades that create expectancy.
- **If win rate is low but avg win ≫ avg loss in R → keep the system**, because expectancy, not reliability, is what pays.
- **If you're obsessing over the entry signal → redirect effort to exits + sizing**, because entry is the 10% pillar (random entry still wins).
- **If a backtest looks perfect → suspect curve-fitting**, because degrees-of-freedom bias fits noise.
- **If you can't state your max tolerable drawdown → you haven't set objectives yet**, because every design choice derives from it.

## Expectancy & R — Quick Reference
- **R** = (entry − stop) × size = your 1R loss.
- **R-multiple** = trade P/L ÷ R. (+$1,000 on $500 risk = +2R.)
- **Expectancy** = mean R = (Win% × avgWinR) − (Loss% × avgLossR).
- **Growth ≈ Expectancy × Opportunity − Cost.**
- Worked: 40% win, +5R / −1R → (0.4×5)−(0.6×1) = **+1.4R/trade**.

## Position-Sizing Models — Pick One
| Model | Formula | Equates… | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Fixed amount | 1 unit per $X | nothing | simplest accounts |
| 2 — Equal value | equity ÷ N | dollar exposure | stock portfolios |
| 3 — **Percent Risk (CPR)** | **equity × %risk ÷ R** | **risk** | professional default |
| 4 — Percent Volatility | equity × %vol ÷ (ATR × pt) | volatility | noise-balanced books |

**CPR example**: $50k × 2.5% = $1,250 ÷ $1,000 R = **1 contract** (gold); ÷ $250 R = **5 contracts** (corn).

## The Six Keys (first 4 = expectancy, last 2 = sizing)
1. Reliability (% wins) · 2. Relative size of wins/losses (R) · 3. Cost · 4. Opportunity · 5. Capital · 6. Position sizing.

## Bias Tells & Smells (recognize the situation)
| You notice… | Likely bias | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Can't take the loss | Need to be right / protection | Honor the stop |
| Taking small profits fast | Need to be right | Let winners run / trail |
| All effort on entry | Lotto bias | Move effort to exits + sizing |
| "It's due to bounce" | Gambler's fallacy | Trust the system, not streaks |
| Adding rules to fix backtest | Degrees-of-freedom | Simplify; out-of-sample test |
| "Markets must be orderly" | Randomness / need-to-understand | Accept randomness |

## Golden Rule of Trading
**Cut losses short (stops, Ch 9) + let profits run (exits, Ch 10).** Your edge is created by how you *get out*, not what you buy.
