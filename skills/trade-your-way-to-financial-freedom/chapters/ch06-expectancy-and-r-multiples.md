# Chapter 6: Understanding Expectancy and Other Keys to Trading Success

## Core Idea
Success is governed by **six variables**, not by being right. The first four combine into **expectancy** — the average amount you make (or lose) per dollar risked, over many trades — and expectancy multiplied by **opportunity** (frequency) is what actually grows your account.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Six Keys to Investment Success**:
  1. **Reliability** — % of trades that win ("hit rate").
  2. **Relative size of wins vs. losses** — best measured as **R-multiples**, not raw averages.
  3. **Cost of trading** — commissions + slippage (a constant drag).
  4. **Opportunity** — how often you get to trade.
  5. **Size of capital** — how the first four scale against your account.
  6. **Position-sizing model** — how many units you trade ("how much?").
  - First **four** → **expectancy**. Last **two** → **position sizing / money management** (Ch 12).
- **R-multiple**: Express every trade's result as a multiple of its **initial risk (R)**. If you risk $500 (your stop distance × size), a $1,000 gain = **+2R**, a $5,000 gain = **+10R**, a $1,000 loss = **−2R**.
  - When to use: To evaluate trades and systems on a common, risk-normalized scale.
  - How: R = the predefined amount you'll lose if your stop is hit. Record each closed trade's profit/loss ÷ R.
- **Expectancy = the mean (average) R-multiple** of your system's distribution of trades. It tells you what you can expect to make *per dollar risked* over many trades.
- **Expectancy × Opportunity**: A positive expectancy only compounds if you get enough trades. Expectancy of +0.2R means little at 10 trades/year, a lot at 10 trades/day.

## Key Concepts
- **Initial risk (R)**: Your worst-case loss per unit, set by your stop at entry.
- **Expectancy (formula form)**: `Expectancy = (Win% × avg win in R) − (Loss% × avg loss in R)` = mean R per trade.
- **Distribution of R-multiples**: A system *is* its set of R-multiples; reliability and average win/loss are just summaries of that distribution.

## Mental Models
- Stop asking "Will this trade be right?" Ask "What's the **expectancy**, and how many **opportunities** do I get?"
- Think in **R**, not dollars: it makes wins/losses comparable across trades of different size and decouples evaluation from account size.

## Worked Example — The Snow-Fight Metaphor & an expectancy calc
**Snow fight**: you hide behind a snow wall. White snowballs (winners) add to it; black snowballs (losers) blast holes. What protects the wall is (a) the *percentage* that are white (reliability) **and** (b) their *relative size* — golf-ball whites vs. 6-foot-boulder blacks will destroy the wall even if most snowballs are white. This is exactly reliability × relative R-size.

**Calc**: A trend system wins 40% of the time. Winners average +5R, losers average −1R.
`Expectancy = (0.40 × 5) − (0.60 × 1) = 2.0 − 0.6 = +1.4R per trade.`
At 50 trades/year you risk-weighted earn ~70R/year; at 200 trades/year, ~280R/year — same expectancy, **opportunity** changes the outcome. Note the 40% win rate would horrify a "need to be right" trader, yet the system is excellent.

## Key Takeaways
1. Six keys drive success; "being right" (reliability) is only one of them.
2. Expectancy = **mean R-multiple** = expected profit per dollar risked.
3. Always evaluate trades and systems in **R**, not dollars.
4. A low-win-rate system can have high expectancy if winners are large in R.
5. **Expectancy × opportunity** (frequency) determines actual account growth.
