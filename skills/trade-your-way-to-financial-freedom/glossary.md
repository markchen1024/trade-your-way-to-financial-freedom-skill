# Glossary — Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom

**Arbitrage** — Exploiting pricing/structural discrepancies between related instruments; edges are real but get competed away (Ch 5).

**Average True Range (ATR)** — Wilder's measure of daily price range including gaps; the volatility input to volatility-based stops and Model 4 sizing (Ch 9, Ch 12).

**Concept** — The objectively measurable idea a system exploits (trend, reversion, seasonality, etc.); step 6 of the 12-step model (Ch 4, Ch 5).

**Conservatism bias** — Failing to update a belief when new evidence contradicts it; possibly the root of all biases (Ch 2).

**Cost of trading** — Commissions + slippage; a constant destructive drag on each trade; key #3 of six (Ch 6, Ch 11).

**CPR model** — "Cash × Percent-risk ÷ Risk" — the percent-risk position-sizing formula (Model 3) (Ch 12).

**Degrees-of-freedom bias** — Over-optimizing/curve-fitting by adding parameters until a system fits history and fails live (Ch 2).

**Discipline** — The execution glue; one of the three keys to making it all work (Ch 1, Ch 13).

**Drawdown** — Peak-to-trough equity decline; must be tolerable both financially and emotionally (Ch 3).

**Expectancy** — The mean (average) R-multiple of a system's trade distribution; expected profit per dollar risked over many trades (Ch 6).

**Expectancy × Opportunity** — Expectancy times trade frequency; what actually grows the account (Ch 6, Ch 11).

**Filter** — A continuous screen on the trading universe (e.g., price > 200-day MA); distinct from a discrete setup (Ch 7).

**Gambler's fallacy bias** — Believing a win/loss is "due" after a streak; a consequence of the randomness bias (Ch 2).

**Golden rule of trading** — Cut your losses short (Ch 9) and let your profits run (Ch 10); created by how you *exit*, not what you buy.

**Hit rate** — See *Reliability*.

**Holy Grail** — Metaphor (per Joseph Campbell) for finding *yourself*; the trading edge is internal mastery, not a magic system (Ch 1).

**Initial risk (R)** — Worst-case loss per unit, set by the stop at entry; the denominator of every R-multiple (Ch 6, Ch 9).

**Lotto bias** — Obsessing over entry because it feels controllable, while neglecting exits and sizing (Ch 2, Ch 8).

**Low-risk idea** — A trade idea with positive expectancy and risk small enough to survive worst-case in the short term; the one trait all of Tharp's model traders shared (Ch 1).

**Modeling** — Extracting a teachable model from the *common elements* across many experts (Ch 1).

**Need to be right** — The most destructive live-trading bias; makes traders cut winners and hold losers (Ch 2).

**Need-to-understand bias** — Imposing order/reasons on largely random market data (Ch 2).

**Neural networks** — Pattern-fitting models with high curve-fitting (degrees-of-freedom) risk (Ch 5).

**Opportunity factor** — How often a system gives you a trade; key #4 of six; multiplies expectancy (Ch 6, Ch 11).

**Percent Risk Model (Model 3)** — Risk a fixed % of equity per trade: units = equity × %risk ÷ R; first model that *equates risk* (Ch 12).

**Percent Volatility Model (Model 4)** — Size so each position's volatility (ATR) is a fixed % of equity (Ch 12).

**Position sizing** — The algorithm answering "how much?"; ~30% of trading success; the lever that meets objectives (Ch 1, Ch 12).

**Postdictive error bias** — Using future information (unavailable at the time) in a backtest, producing fantasy results (Ch 2).

**Profit-taking exit** — The rule that closes a winning position; where the big-R winners (and expectancy) come from (Ch 10).

**Protection bias** — Failing to take small losses / not honoring a stop; quietly destroys accounts (Ch 2, Ch 9).

**Protective (initial) stop** — Predefined exit that caps worst-case loss and defines R (Ch 9).

**R / R-multiple** — A trade's result expressed as a multiple of its initial risk R (e.g., a $1,000 gain on $500 risk = +2R) (Ch 6).

**Random entry** — Coin-flip entry used as the benchmark any real entry must beat; profitable with good exits + sizing (Ch 8).

**Randomness bias** — Refusing to accept the market's large random component; wanting predictable order (Ch 2).

**Reliability** — % of trades that win ("hit rate"); key #1 of six — one factor, not the most important (Ch 6).

**Representation bias** — Treating a representation (e.g., a price bar) as the actual thing it stands for (Ch 2).

**Reliability bias** — Assuming data/indicators are accurate and complete when they aren't (Ch 2).

**Setup** — A condition that must be present before an entry is allowed; the "stalking" stage (Ch 7).

**Six keys to investment success** — Reliability, relative size of wins/losses (R), cost, opportunity, capital, position sizing (Ch 6).

**Slippage** — The difference between expected and actual fill price; part of trading cost (Ch 11).

**Snow-fight metaphor** — Illustrates that both the % of winners *and* their relative size determine whether your "wall" survives (Ch 6).

**Stalking** — Shortening your time frame after a setup to find a lower-risk entry (Ch 7).

**Time frame** — Your intended holding period and the effort you can devote; shapes every downstream choice (Ch 3, Ch 4).

**Trailing stop** — A stop that moves only in the favorable direction, locking in open profit while letting winners run (Ch 10).

**Trend following** — Riding sustained moves; low win rate, large R-multiples; Tharp's favored concept for most traders (Ch 5).

**12-step model** — Tharp's ordered system-development checklist (inventory → … → worst-case rehearsal) (Ch 4).

**Worst-case scenario planning** — Mentally rehearsing disasters and your responses before they occur; step 12 (Ch 4, Ch 13).
