# Chapter 10: How to Take Profits

## Core Idea
Exits — not entries — make your money. Profit-taking exits are the other half of the golden rule: *let your profits run*. Together with stops (Ch 9), exits determine your distribution of R-multiples and therefore your expectancy.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Exits create expectancy**: Since stops cut losers at ~−1R, it is the *profit exits* that produce the large-R winners that make a system positive. Tharp: the golden rule of trading is created by how you **get out**, not by what you buy.
- **Purposes of a profit-taking exit**: (1) let winners run to capture big R-multiples, (2) protect accumulated open profit, (3) get out when the trade premise is exhausted.
- **Trailing stops**: Ratchet the exit in the trade's favor (e.g., 3× volatility trailing) so you stay in trends but lock in gains as price moves — the engine behind the coin-flip system's profits (Ch 8).
- **Simplicity and multiple exits**: Prefer a few simple, logical exits; you may split a position and use different exits for different purposes (e.g., partial profit target + trailing stop on the remainder).

## Key Concepts
- **Profit-taking exit**: The rule that closes a winning position.
- **Trailing stop**: A stop that moves only in the favorable direction, never against you.
- **Profit objective/target**: A fixed level to take profit (simple, but caps your big winners).
- **Letting profits run**: Holding winners for large R-multiples instead of banking small gains.

## Mental Models
- Treat exits as where the edge lives: design exits first-class, entries last (consistent with the 12-step order).
- Resist the **need to be right** that screams "take the small profit now" — capping winners kills expectancy.

## Anti-patterns
- **Taking profits too early**: Selling winners to feel "right" — turns a +10R potential into +1R and wrecks expectancy.
- **Tight profit targets on a trend system**: Caps the big winners the whole concept depends on.
- **Over-complicated exits**: Many fragile exit rules = curve-fitting; prefer simple, robust ones.
- **What to avoid**: Letting a winner turn into a loser by having no trailing protection.

## Worked Example
A trend trade enters long at $50 with a stop at $47 (R = $3). Instead of a fixed target, the trader uses a **3× volatility trailing stop**. Price runs to $80; the trailing stop ratchets up behind it and finally exits at $74 — a **+8R winner** ($24 ÷ $3). Had the trader taken a "sensible" +2R profit at $56 (the need to be right), they'd have captured $6 instead of $24 and starved the system of the large winners that make its 40% win rate profitable. The exit, not the entry, produced the result.

## Key Takeaways
1. Exits make the money; design them before entries.
2. "Let profits run" (this chapter) + "cut losses short" (Ch 9) = the golden rule.
3. Trailing stops keep you in trends while protecting open profit.
4. Taking profits too early — to be "right" — is a top expectancy killer.
5. Keep exits simple and logical; complexity is curve-fitting.
